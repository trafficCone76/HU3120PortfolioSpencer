1. What changes did you make to the assignment? Why? (I'm looking for more than "because Jordan said so." Try to think about the assignment's audience and how revisions would help them.)
For the proposal, I revised grammar and spelling to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the document. I had not noticed that the proposal had zero sections that mentioned the figures, leaving it all up to the primary audience to infer their use. Pointing this out in the feedback let me know to add mentions of the figures at appropriate points in the text. I ensured that I would have the references match the content they support. I also added clarifying sentences in multiple sections to reduce the feeling of clunkiness and enhance overall readability, making the transitions between sections smoother and more natural. 1. What changes did you make to the assignment? Why? (I'm looking for more than "because Jordan said so." Try to think about the assignment's audience and how revisions would help them.)
I fixed a large number of grammatical errors and misspellings. I also changed the steps to show “Step x” and “substep x” based on the feedback. This did lead to issues, as mentioned in emails, when the clarifications are added to the numbering system word automatically adds a large amount of padding between the "step x" and step description. On top of this, I bolded the Steps to help readability and added a space after each step; no space was added between step and substep to help the connection between each step be made subconsciously.

2. Were there any changes that you received feedback on and didn't incorporate? Why?
I did not incorporate the feedback to add images to the major instructions. I decided against this as, while it would be helpful, I ran into a multitude of issues getting the formatting and flow for the document to not get interrupted or fully break with the added images. For example, I tried to add an image of the shifter so the reader would EXACTLY what to interact with. When adding it, I tried adding it in-line, but it broke the flow of the document, and when adding to the sides, it got confusing which image correlated to which step. I also had my girlfriend run through the steps, one version with the images, and one without. The feedback I received was that the steps without images were easier to go through, but harder to visualize what was needed. She also mentioned the version with images containing too many images, while helpful, it made it confusing exactly what she was looking for in each image. She also mentioned she still preferred it without images. Thus, I chose to omit images, whilst I understand the document would be better with images, I have not figured out how to properly implement images without cluttering the document or making it worse to go through.

Style Guide Reflection

The style guide I created works well enough for a majority of the assignments. The font worked incredibly well, and it still is my favorite font for readability and accessibility. I believe I made the correct choice with Courier New. I also believe the line spacing was the correct choice. The simple colors allowed good contrast and accessibility. Now, onto the reason I say it worked only for a majority of the assignments. This is because of the major instructions assignment. This assignment was not very easily readable, and to have good flow and readability, more padding/margin is required. Also, allowing more formatting freedoms for differentiating steps and substeps is required. This assignment specifically would have benefited from more freedom with line spacing, as having 1.15 line spacing specifically between each step would have been beneficial. I also ran into issues with image formatting, which I should have included in the style guide. I also would have chosen to make more distinctions on what counts as a heading vs body, as it got confusing on assignments. Especially on this website, it has been extremely difficult to get the formatting to exactly match as “font sizes” and such do not mean the same thing as they do in a word processor, nor do they scale the same.

I believe that this style guide would work well in a job setting, largely due to the focus on readability. My field is currently aging, so the distribution between younger and older engineers is proportionally skewed towards the upper end. Meaning larger text, consistent spacing, and contrasted coloring, ensure the style guide will do well.
