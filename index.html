1. What changes did you make to the assignment? Why? (I'm looking for more than "because Jordan said so." Try to think about the assignment's audience and how revisions would help them.)
For the proposal, I revised grammar and spelling to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the document. I had not noticed that the proposal had zero sections that mentioned the figures, leaving it all up to the primary audience inferring their use. Pointing this out in the feedback let me know to add mentions of the figures at appropriate points in the text. I did ensure I would have the references match the content they support. Doing this had me find out that the current locations of the figures felt more random than I expected. To remedy this I moved the locations of the figures to better reflect when I mentioned them in the writing and where they would support the section nearest, improving the flow and coherence of the figures. I also added clarifying sentences in multiple sections to reduce the feeling of clunkiness and enhance overall readability, making the transitions between sections smoother and more natural. I also found I never fully explained why I chose the route itself. Thus I implemented clarifications for my reasonings in the solution section of the proposal.

1. What changes did you make to the assignment? Why? (I'm looking for more than "because Jordan said so." Try to think about the assignment's audience and how revisions would help them.)
I fixed a large number of grammatical errors and misspellings. I also changed the steps to show “Step x” and “substep x” based on the feedback. This did lead to issues, as mentioned in emails, when the clarifications are added to the numbering system word automatically adds a large amount of padding between the "step x" and step description. On top of this, I bolded the Steps to help readability and added a space after each step; no space was added between step and substep to help the connection between each step be made subconsciously.

2. Were there any changes that you received feedback on and didn't incorporate? Why?
I did not incorporate the feedback to add images to the major instructions. I decided against this as, while it would be helpful, I ran into a multitude of issues getting the formatting and flow for the document to not get interrupted or fully break with the added images. For example I tried to add an image of the shifter so the reader would EXACTLY what to interact with. When adding it I tried adding it inline but it broke the flow of the document, and when adding to the sides it got confusing which image correlated to which step. I also had my girlfriend run through the steps, one version with the images, and one without. The feedback I received was that the steps without images were easier to go through, but harder to visualize what was needed. She also mentioned the version with images containing too many images, while helpful, it made it confusing exactly what she was looking for in each image. She also mentioned she still preferred it without images. Thus I chose to omit images, whilst I understand the document would be better with images, I have not figured out how to properly implement images without cluttering the document or making it worse to go through.
